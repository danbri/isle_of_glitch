# How to Play Glo-ball Gopher

You're a glowing gopher courier, bouncing between airports worldwide on electromagnetic trampolines, delivering mysterious packages against the clock.

---

## The One Button

The game has **one main control**: the bounce button (bottom-right **⚡** on mobile, **SPACE** on desktop).

**How it works:**

| Action | What happens |
|--------|-------------|
| **Tap** (<200ms) | Scenic Hop — gentle low arc, good for nearby targets |
| **Hold** (200–600ms) | Express Arc — powerful high bounce, medium distance |
| **Long hold** (>600ms) | Night Glide — fast and low, covers huge distance |

Hold the button to charge. A trajectory arc appears showing where you'll go. Release to launch.

The charge ring around the button changes color:
- **Blue** = under-charged (won't reach the target)
- **Green** = dialed in (good power for this distance)
- **Orange** = over-charged (you'll overshoot)

### Rapid-Tap Boost (the fun one)

When you're **already in the air**, quick taps give speed boosts instead of full bounces:

- Each tap adds ~20% speed in your travel direction
- Consecutive taps stack: BOOST! → BOOST x2! → BOOST x3! → TURBO!
- The pitch rises with each tap — very satisfying
- This sends you soaring high and far in a dramatic arc

**This is the most fun input in the game.** Charge-launch, then hammer the button mid-flight for escalating boosts. You'll arc spectacularly over entire continents. The downside: you'll probably overshoot your target by thousands of kilometers. Worth it aesthetically. Bad for your score.

### Unstick Kick

If you're floating nearly stationary in the air (speed ≤ 0.3), a quick tap gives you an upward kick instead of a boost — an escape hatch so you never get stuck.

## Steering

You have limited mid-air steering:

| Input | Effect |
|-------|--------|
| **WASD** or **Arrow keys** (desktop) | Nudge your trajectory left/right/forward/back |
| **Swipe** (mobile) | Analog steering — swipe distance = force |

Steering is subtle. It won't turn you around, but it can nudge you onto target for the last few hundred kilometers.

**On the ground**, WASD/swipe rotates your **aim direction** for the next bounce.

## The Delivery Loop

1. **Choose a delivery** — three options appear:
   - 🟢 **Short** (green) — nearby, generous timer, lower points
   - 🟣 **Medium** (purple) — mid-range, normal timer
   - 🟠 **Long** (orange) — far away, tight timer, big points

2. **Follow the arrow** — a cyan arrow at the top of the screen always points toward your target, with distance in km and a progress bar that turns green (close) then gold (very close)

3. **Land near the target rings** — three concentric rings mark the delivery zone:
   - **Outer ring** (blue, ~955 km radius) — 1× score
   - **Middle ring** (purple, ~510 km) — 2× score (PRECISE)
   - **Inner ring** (cyan, pulsing, ~319 km) — 3× score (BULLSEYE)

4. **Pick your next delivery** — the faster you chain deliveries, the higher your combo multiplier (up to 5×)

A 3-minute session clock starts when you pick your first delivery. Beat your high score.

---

## Scoring Quick Reference

```
Score = (base + time bonus + distance bonus + streak) × accuracy × combo
```

- **Accuracy**: BULLSEYE 3×, PRECISE 2×, DELIVERED 1×
- **Combo**: deliver within 15 seconds of your last delivery → multiplier climbs (2×, 3×... 5×)
- **Streak**: +15 pts per consecutive delivery (caps at +200)
- **Chain launch**: bounce within 3 seconds of delivering → 1.3× force bonus

Timeout = −100 pts and combo reset. Don't dawdle.

## Strategy Tips

**For high scores:**
- Pick SHORT deliveries and chain them fast — combo multiplier matters more than distance bonus
- Use medium-charge Express Arcs — they're the sweet spot of distance vs. control
- Watch the charge ring color: release when it's green
- Steer into the target on approach — even small nudges help

**For fun:**
- Pick LONG deliveries, charge big, then rapid-tap boost mid-flight
- Watch yourself arc over entire oceans and continents
- Try to spot coastlines, city clusters, and the ISS during high arcs
- The aurora glows near the poles — bounce toward Scandinavia or Antarctica

**For not getting lost:**
- The direction arrow is your best friend — always visible at the top of the screen
- The progress bar beneath it shows how far you've come
- If you overshoot, you'll see "OVERSHOOT!" — steer back or wait to land and re-launch

## Other Controls

| Input | What it does |
|-------|-------------|
| **☰** (top-right) | Hamburger menu — camera modes, teleport, audio toggle |
| **H** | Toggle debug panel + FPS graph |
| **M** | Toggle Ship's Computer (NAV / ORBIT / DEBUG tabs) |
| Click/tap an airport | Set it as your aim target |

### Camera Modes (via ☰ menu)

- **Follow Ship** — default, tracks your courier
- **Free Camera** — orbital controls, drag to look around
- **ISS views** — watch from the International Space Station (→ Europe, → Ship, → Earth Center, → Aurora)

### Ship's Computer (M key)

A draggable panel with three tabs:
- **NAV** — navigation data
- **ORBIT** — orbital parameters
- **DEBUG** — live sliders for tweaking physics and visuals
