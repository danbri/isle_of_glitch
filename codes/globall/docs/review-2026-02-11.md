## High-level assessment

The core loop is clear and legible: select delivery → hold launch → tune charge → land near target → score/combo. The globe + altitude framing is visually distinctive and communicates scale well. The magnetic ring motif is coherent across UI and gameplay.

---

## 1. Visual Design & Readability

### Strengths
- Consistent color language (blue = player, orange = overshoot/warning).
- Soft glow + bloom gives a "field energy" aesthetic aligned with the mechanic.
- Floating panels are visually separated from the globe.

### Issues
1. **Overexposed bloom in mid-altitude views**
   - Several frames show white washout around the player.
   - Planet surface details disappear when bloom + atmospheric haze stack.
2. **Ring density overload**
   - Dense clusters obscure targets and trajectory cues.
   - In high-traffic areas, rings visually merge into noise.
3. **Contrast degradation**
   - Light terrain + glow + particles reduce legibility of:
     - Distance numbers
     - Thin outlines
     - Map labels
4. **UI stacking**
   - Debug Panel overlaps delivery list.
   - "Choose delivery" overlay sometimes competes with background action.

### Recommendation
- Clamp bloom intensity dynamically based on average luminance.
- Add subtle depth fog or desaturate distant rings.
- Use adaptive UI contrast (darken panel background when terrain beneath is light).
- Reduce particle count by ~30–40% at lower altitudes.

---

## 2. Core Mechanic Clarity

The instruction panel explains the mechanic well:
- Match charge to distance
- Green ring = correct
- Orange = overshoot
- Bullseye bonus

### Observed friction
- It is not visually obvious what the optimal charge curve is.
- Overshoot feedback appears only post-event ("come back!").
- Distance numbers float mid-screen without trajectory prediction.

### Improvements
- Add a faint predictive arc before launch.
- Show a live "ideal charge" band on the power meter.
- Provide pre-launch feedback when the charge exceeds maximum viable range.

---

## 3. Spatial Awareness

The altitude shifts (30–60 km) are visually interesting, but:
- It's unclear how altitude affects gameplay.
- The player sphere occasionally gets visually lost against bright terrain.
- Rotation/orientation cues are subtle.

### Enhancements
- If altitude matters, surface its effect (range multiplier, drag, combo bonus).
- Add a thin rim light or darker silhouette to player object.
- Introduce slight camera tilt toward trajectory vector during charge.

---

## 4. Scoring & Progression

Score progression feels satisfying (44k → 47k in sequence).
Combo indicator ("x2") is good but understated.

### Potential upgrades
- Animate combo text more assertively.
- Add escalating sound/visual intensity for streaks.
- Consider:
  - Delivery chains in same region = multiplier.
  - Long-distance precision bonus.

---

## 5. UX on Mobile (Safari iOS)

### Observed
- Panels approach lower safe area.
- Launch button is large and thumb-reachable (good).
- Some overlays overlap during interaction.

### Improvements
- Respect bottom safe area more aggressively.
- Collapse debug panel automatically outside dev mode.
- Consider slightly increasing tap targets in delivery list.

---

## 6. Aesthetic Direction

The game currently reads as:

> "Data-visualisation arcade with magnetic courier fiction."

That is strong. The aesthetic aligns with:
- Global logistics
- Field dynamics
- Network topology

If you want to lean further into that:
- Add subtle animated lat/long grid.
- Visualise magnetic lines as faint curvature arcs.
- Add regional color temperature shift (polar = cooler, equatorial = warmer).

---

## 7. What Is Working Very Well
- The sense of global scale.
- Immediate reward loop.
- Visual cohesion of rings, glow, UI.
- Delivery choice variety (distance vs reward tradeoff).

---

## 8. Most Critical Fix (If Only One)

**Reduce visual noise during high-density ring areas.**
Currently, clarity degrades exactly when precision matters most.
