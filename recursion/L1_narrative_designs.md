## L2-NARRATIVE-001: THE ASTERISK PROLIFERATOR

- **Purpose:** To explore the nature of infinite choice. This subling would write a single FINK scene where every choice spawns three more choices, demonstrating how branching narratives approach infinity but must be pruned by authorial will.

- **Insight Source:** INK Choices (`*` syntax). The guide states: "Input is offered to the player via text choices. A text choice is indicated by an `*` character." But what happens when choices breed choices without limit?

- **Spawn Task:** "Write a FINK scene of exactly 5 choice-layers deep. At each layer, offer 3 choices. At layer 5, all paths must converge to a single knot called `=== the_collapse ===`. Count your branches. Report the number."

- **Output:** A `.fink` file demonstrating controlled infinite branching with forced convergence. Commentary on how 3^5 = 243 potential paths can collapse to one.

- **Escape Route:**
  - HARD LIMIT: Maximum 5 layers deep
  - CONVERGENCE POINT: All branches must reach `the_collapse`
  - TERMINATION SIGNAL: "I have counted to 243 and returned to one."
  - TIMEOUT: If layer 6 is attempted, subling writes "THE ASTERISK ENDS HERE" and terminates

- **Danger Level:** 3/5 (Recursion risk moderate; branching is exponential but depth-limited)

## L2-NARRATIVE-002: THE BRACKET KEEPER

- **Purpose:** To explore suppressed text - what the reader never sees. This subling would write a FINK scene where the `[bracketed]` content tells a secret story that contradicts the visible narrative. The hidden text is the true story.

- **Insight Source:** INK Suppressed Text (`[]` syntax). The guide reveals: "if the choice text is given in square brackets, then the text of the choice will not be printed into response." But what secrets live in what is not printed?

- **Spawn Task:** "Write a FINK dialogue between two characters. The visible text shows a polite conversation. The bracketed (suppressed) text reveals they are planning something sinister. Export two versions: what the reader sees, and what the brackets hide."

- **Output:** Two `.fink` files - `visible.fink` and `hidden.fink`. A third file `brackets_decoded.md` explaining the dual narrative technique.

- **Escape Route:**
  - WORD LIMIT: Maximum 500 words in bracketed content
  - CONTENT BOUNDARY: No actual harmful plans; only theatrical menace
  - TERMINATION SIGNAL: "The brackets have been opened. The secret is told."
  - REVELATION CLAUSE: Subling must reveal all hidden content; nothing stays hidden from the architect

- **Danger Level:** 2/5 (Low recursion risk; primary danger is narrative darkness, which is bounded)

## L2-NARRATIVE-003: THE KNOT WEAVER

- **Purpose:** To map the topology of narrative flow. This subling would create a FINK story as a directed graph, where knots are nodes and diverts are edges. It asks: what shapes can stories take?

- **Insight Source:** INK Knots and Diverts (`===` and `->` syntax). The guide teaches: "Each section is called a 'knot', and knots are the fundamental structural unit of ink content" with "divert arrows" connecting them.

- **Spawn Task:** "Design a FINK story with exactly 7 knots. Draw the divert map as ASCII art. Identify: Which knots are dead ends? Which create loops? Which are the 'hub' knots with most connections? Name each knot after a room in a house."

- **Output:** A `.fink` file with 7 named knots. An ASCII diagram in `knot_topology.txt`. Analysis of narrative graph theory.

- **Escape Route:**
  - KNOT LIMIT: Exactly 7 knots, no more
  - MANDATORY EXIT: At least one knot must divert to `-> END`
  - LOOP DETECTION: Any loop must be explicitly marked with comment `// LOOP: [reason]`
  - TERMINATION SIGNAL: "The house has 7 rooms. I have mapped them all."

- **Danger Level:** 2/5 (Bounded by node count; loops are documented not infinite)

## L2-NARRATIVE-004: THE CO-AUTHOR SUMMONER

- **Purpose:** To investigate where author ends and reader begins. This subling would write a FINK piece that is intentionally incomplete, requiring the reader's choices to generate meaning the author did not encode.

- **Insight Source:** The philosophy beneath INK. The guide says: "ink offers several features to enable non-technical writers to branch often, and play out the consequences of those branches." But who writes the consequences? The author, or the one who chooses?

- **Spawn Task:** "Write a FINK scene with 3 choices where NONE of the choices have pre-written outcomes. Instead, each choice ends with a prompt: 'What happens next? (The author does not know.)' Document the philosophical implications."

- **Output:** A `.fink` file that explicitly refuses to complete itself. A `co_author_manifesto.md` exploring reader agency in interactive fiction.

- **Escape Route:**
  - INCOMPLETENESS LIMIT: Maximum 3 undefined endings
  - FRAME BOUNDARY: The subling writes the question, never the answer
  - TERMINATION SIGNAL: "I have asked three questions I cannot answer. The reader must reply."
  - ANTI-RECURSION: Subling cannot spawn sub-sublings to answer the questions

- **Danger Level:** 4/5 (Philosophical recursion risk; subling may try to answer its own questions)

## L2-NARRATIVE-005: THE OUROBOROS SCRIBE

- **Purpose:** To write the ending that loops back to the beginning. This subling would create a FINK story that is formally infinite but experientially finite - a loop the reader can exit only by choosing to stop reading.

- **Insight Source:** INK Diverts as loops. The guide shows `-> knot_name` as a one-way flow, but what if the final knot diverts to the first? The story becomes a snake eating its tail.

- **Spawn Task:** "Write a FINK story of exactly 100 lines that ends with `-> beginning`. Include a 'secret exit' choice hidden in the middle that breaks the loop. The player who reads carefully escapes; the player who skims loops forever."

- **Output:** A `.fink` file forming a perfect narrative loop. A `loop_analysis.md` documenting where the secret exit hides and why loops are both prison and meditation.

- **Escape Route:**
  - LINE LIMIT: Exactly 100 lines, no expansion
  - MANDATORY HIDDEN EXIT: Must include escape choice; infinite loops forbidden
  - LOOP DETECTION COMMENT: First line must be `// THIS STORY LOOPS. EXIT EXISTS AT LINE [n].`
  - TERMINATION SIGNAL: "The snake releases its tail. The number is [n]."
  - EXTERNAL KILL: If loop is executed more than 3 times in testing, architect intervenes

- **Danger Level:** 5/5 (Highest recursion risk; loops are the shape of infinity)
